## Why Profit Margins Drop

### Problem: Commands Bundle is Expensive

**Commands (1 credit = 3 commands):**
- Your cost: $0.005 √ó 3 = **$0.015**
- You charge: $0.011-0.013 per credit
- **You LOSE money on commands!** ($0.015 cost vs $0.011-0.013 revenue)

**This is the main issue:** Commands cost more than you charge per credit.

---

## Revised Cost Analysis

### Option 1: Current Proposal (Commands = 1 credit for 3)
**Problem:** Commands cost $0.015 but you only charge $0.011-0.013
**Result:** Negative margin on commands, dragging down overall profit

### Option 2: Adjust Command Pricing
**Solution:** Make commands cost more, or reduce bundle size

**Better Structure:**
- Commands: **1 credit = 2 commands** (instead of 3)
  - Cost: $0.005 √ó 2 = $0.010
  - Revenue: $0.011-0.013
  - **Profit: $0.001-0.003 per credit** ‚úÖ

## Recommended Structure Adjustments

### Option A: Keep 3 Commands but Increase Price
- Commands: 1 credit = 3 commands (but increase package prices)
- AI: 1 credit per use
- Complex: 2 credits per use

**Required price increase:** ~15-20% to maintain margins

### Option B: Reduce Command Bundle
- Commands: **1 credit = 2 commands** (more sustainable)

**This maintains better profit margins**

### Option C: Separate Command Pricing
- Commands: **0.5 credits per command** (or 2 commands = 1 credit)
